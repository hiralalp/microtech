Product Overview

Monarch Stainless Steel Co. supplies comprehensive range of Carbon Steel Pipe Fittings manufactured from high-quality ASTM A234 WPB, ASTM A420 WPL6, and ASTM A105 materials. Our carbon steel fittings offer excellent strength, durability, and cost-effectiveness for general industrial, hydrocarbon processing, and power generation applications.

Available in seamless and welded construction, our fittings include butt weld types (elbows, tees, reducers, caps, stub ends), socket weld fittings, and threaded fittings manufactured to ASME B16.9, ASME B16.11, and MSS SP-75 standards. Sizes range from 1/2" to 48" in various schedules including SCH 40, SCH 80, SCH 160, and XXS.

Each fitting undergoes comprehensive quality testing including dimensional verification, hydrostatic testing, and non-destructive examination. Suitable for temperatures ranging from -29°C to 400°C depending on grade. Available with black, galvanized, or epoxy coating finishes for enhanced corrosion protection.

  • Tees: Equal & Reducing
  • Reducers: Concentric & Eccentric
  • Caps & End Caps
  • Stub Ends

  • Couplings, Unions, Bushings

Material Grades

  • ASTM A234 WPB (Standard)

Standards

  • ASME B16.9 (Butt Weld Fittings)
  • ASME B16.11 (Socket Weld & Threaded)

Key Features & Benefits

Cost-Effective Solution

Most economical piping material for non-corrosive applications. Lower material and fabrication costs compared to alloy and stainless steels.

High Strength

Excellent mechanical properties with good tensile strength and toughness. Suitable for high-pressure and high-temperature applications.

Easy Fabrication & Welding

Excellent weldability with all standard welding processes. Easy to cut, form, and install using conventional tools and techniques.

Wide Availability

Readily available in all sizes and schedules. Quick delivery times and competitive pricing due to widespread production.

Temperature Range

WPB grade suitable for temperatures up to 400°C. WPL6 maintains ductility at low temperatures down to -46°C.

Coating Options

Available with galvanized or epoxy coatings for enhanced corrosion protection in mildly corrosive environments.
